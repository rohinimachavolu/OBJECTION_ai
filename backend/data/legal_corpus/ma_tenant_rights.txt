Massachusetts Tenant Rights - Habitability
Citation: Massachusetts General Law Chapter 239 Section 8A
Jurisdiction: Massachusetts
Category: Housing

Landlords must maintain rental properties in habitable condition, including providing heat, hot water, and keeping the property free from pest infestations and dangerous conditions like mold.

Tenants may withhold rent or repair and deduct costs if landlord fails to make necessary repairs after written notice.

Plain English: Your landlord must keep your apartment safe and livable. If they don't fix serious problems like mold after you notify them in writing, you have legal options including withholding rent.
