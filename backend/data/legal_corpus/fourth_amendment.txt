Fourth Amendment - Search and Seizure
Citation: U.S. Constitution, Amendment IV
Jurisdiction: Federal
Category: Criminal, Police

The right of the people to be secure in their persons, houses, papers, and effects, against unreasonable searches and seizures, shall not be violated, and no Warrants shall issue, but upon probable cause.

Plain English: Police cannot search you, your home, or your belongings without a warrant or probable cause. You have the right to refuse consent to searches.
